## Deconstructing the "All Factored In" Methodology
What does it mean to truly "factor in" all elements of the energy story? In mathematics, factoring involves finding what to multiply together to get a given result—breaking a composite number into its prime components. Similarly, Factor This™ deconstructs complex energy developments into their core drivers and implications. This approach ensures that stakeholders are not reacting to isolated events but are understanding the systemic interactions that define the market's trajectory.

### The Core Variables in the Energy Equation
The platform's content consistently analyzes several non-negotiable factors, treating them as interdependent rather than siloed:

1.  **Technology & Innovation:** Coverage extends beyond mainstream deployments to explore frontier ideas, as seen in the reference to "18 Crazy Solar Technology ideas." This includes deep dives into how innovations like agrivoltaics, floating solar, and next-generation battery chemistries change the fundamental economics and feasibility of projects.
2.  **Policy & Regulation:** The impact of legislation like the Inflation Reduction Act, state-level Renewable Portfolio Standards (RPS), and international climate agreements is not reported as political news but analyzed for its direct effect on project finance, development timelines, and market demand.
3.  **Economics & Finance:** "All Factored In" necessitates a clear-eyed look at capital costs, levelized cost of energy (LCOE), financing structures, and commodity prices. The platform examines how interest rates, tax equity, and new financial instruments shape what gets built and where.
4.  **Market Dynamics & Integration:** This involves analyzing transmission congestion, wholesale market design, corporate Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs), and the practical challenges of integrating variable renewable resources into a stable grid—a theme echoed in the research citing that "energy systems can be more like the Internet... but only if they are based on renewable sources."
5.  **Social License & Community Engagement:** The human element is a critical factor. Project success increasingly depends on community benefits, workforce development, and environmental justice considerations, all of which are factored into the platform's reporting on project development.
